Project: Customer Defection & Behavior Analysis
An interactive BI dashboard for telecom analyzing customer behavior to identify key churn drivers and deliver data-driven retention insights.

1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
-High Churn: Losing 26.54% of the customer base (1,869 churned vs. 5,174 retained).
-Financial Risk: Churn threatens a $456.12K monthly revenue stream out of $16.06M total revenue.

2. Key Drivers of Customer Churn
-Contract Type: Month-to-month contracts have a 42.71% churn rate (6.32x more likely to leave). Two-year contracts offer maximum stability with only 2.83% churn.
-Fiber Optic Crisis: Accounts for 41.89% of churn (2.89x higher risk), signaling urgent quality/pricing issues compared to DSL (7.40%).
-Electronic Check: Increases churn likelihood by 2.65x, indicating a flawed billing user experience.

3. Tenure Effect & Customer Loyalty
-The 9-Month Danger Zone: Churn outpaces retention in the initial subscription months:
Months 1–3: 56.21% churn (597 customers) vs. 43.79% retained.
Months 4–9: 50.22% churn (931 customers) vs. 49.78% retained.
-Long-Term Loyalty: Retention peaks at 72 months (73.46%), proving loyalty grows over time.

4. Actionable Recommendations
-Early Retention Campaigns: Deploy proactive support and perks during the first 9 months to save early sign-ups.
-Incentivize Long Contracts: Offer discounts to migrate monthly users into 1 or 2-year agreements.
-Audit Fiber Optic Service: Run immediate technical/pricing surveys for fiber subscribers to fix the 41.89% defection.
-Streamline Payments: Incentivize users to switch from manual electronic checks to automated bank debits (Auto-Debit).
